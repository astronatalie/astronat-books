My Collection

Hi! My name is Natalie and I am a 21-year-old science student and educator from Sydney, Australia. I have been interested in collecting books since high school, where I fell in love with the 1980s Coroneos textbook my Year 11 maths teacher showed my class - something about the typewritten pages littered with hand-drawn diagrams was so much more appealing than the computer-designed textbooks I had for all my other subjects. The first book in my collection was a gift that an ex-boyfriend found on the side of the road - not the most glamourous origin story, but Music of the Spheres is still one of my favourites; again, the beautiful illustrations were what drew me in, and it was only after Murchie made reference to the hypothetical concept of having humans living in orbit that I was prompted to check the publication date. Since then, I've prioritised collecting books that are often overlooked by other collectors, forgotten pieces of history that I can preserve (and spend many hours making a website for!).

My collection currently comprises a couple dozen books on various STEM topics, predominantly astronomy, astrophysics, and the development of modern science, topics which supplement both my undergraduate studies and my work as a STEM educator. I find it invaluable to have access to the history of the modern concepts I hear about in my lectures, as I believe science is at its most valuable when we acknowledge how it has developed over time. To quote my 2023 entry to the Australian Young Book Collector's Prize:

"I collect books to preserve knowledge and use it to inform both myself and others. When my astrophysics homework leaves me lost, I have hundreds of years of background information to set me on the right path. When my young students bombard me with “why” in response to whatever answer I give them, I am prepared to take them on a journey through decades of technological advancement. When the world is faced with uncertainty, I know I have the voices of thousands of academics, scientists, and researchers preserved in ink, and a voice with which to share them."

I hope you enjoy learning about my collection - if you have any questions, you can contact me through @astronat__ on Instagram - I always have a spare hour (or ten) to talk about my books :)

Messel Collection

The published lectures given as part of the Nuclear Research Foundation Summer Science School (NRFSSS), now International Science School (ISS), as founded and lead by Professor Harry Messel, from 1958-1968, as well as additional related books.

Space Collection

A range of books on various areas of astronomy and astrophysics, outlining the evolution of our understanding of the universe. Contains some of my most valuable (sentimentally and monetarily) books.

General Science Collection

Mainly books outlining important historical developments in science, with a healthy mix of other STEM texts. Read: I am indecisive and can't stick to just one area for my collection.
